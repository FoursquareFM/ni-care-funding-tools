About your situation

Temporary stays are treated differently

Your home is usually ignored for a temporary stay, and the Trust may use a different charging approach at first. The rules can change if a temporary stay becomes permanent.

Financial thresholds (2025/26)

These are the key boundaries used in Northern Ireland's financial assessment for care. If your total assets (capital) are under £14,250, the Trust usually covers your care. Between £14,250 and £23,250, you contribute on a sliding scale. Above £23,250, you're expected to fund your own care unless Continuing Healthcare applies.

Self-funding does not mean any fee is sustainable

Having savings above the upper threshold means you pay your own care fees for now, but this does not guarantee you can afford any care home long-term. Consider whether the fee level is sustainable as your savings reduce. If you choose a home that charges more than the Trust's usual rate, you or your family may face third-party top-up pressure later if Trust support is needed. Think carefully about future affordability, not just what you can pay today.

Property may affect your capital assessment

You've indicated you own property but have low savings. Be aware that if you move into permanent residential or nursing care, your property may be counted as capital after the first 12 weeks — unless a qualifying person (spouse, child under 16, disabled or elderly relative) still lives there. This could move you above the £23,250 threshold and make you a self-funder. The Trust will assess this as part of the financial assessment.
