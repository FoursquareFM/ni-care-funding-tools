About your situation

This information helps identify which funding options may be available for your situation

Temporary stays are treated differently

Your home is usually ignored for a temporary stay, and the Trust may use a different charging approach at first. The rules can change if a temporary stay becomes permanent.

Financial thresholds (2025/26)

These are the key boundaries used in Northern Ireland's financial assessment for care. If your assessable capital is under £14,250, the Trust usually covers your care. Between £14,250 and £23,250, you contribute on a sliding scale. Above £23,250, you would need to fund your care privately unless Continuing Healthcare applies.

What this means for you

Having savings above the upper threshold means you would fund your own care fees at this stage. This does not mean any fee is sustainable long-term. Consider whether the fee level will remain affordable as your savings reduce. If you choose a home that charges more than the Trust's usual rate, you or your family may face third-party top-up costs later if Trust support is needed. Think carefully about future affordability, not just what you can pay today.
